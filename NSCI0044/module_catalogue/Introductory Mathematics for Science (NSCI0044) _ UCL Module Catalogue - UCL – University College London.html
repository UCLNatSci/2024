Introductory Mathematics for Science (NSCI0044)


    

        
Key information

        
        
Faculty

              
Faculty of Mathematical and Physical Sciences

              
Teaching department

              
Natural Sciences

              
Credit value

              
15

            
Restrictions

              

                This module is suitable for students with no prior background in A-level mathematics or its equivalent.

                                    Link to online timetable

Description

                

  

    

      In this module, you will be introduced to mathematical methods that are widely used in the sciences. Through a combination of lectures and feedback classes, you will explore core topics including functions, exponentials, logarithms, units and orders of magnitude, and rates of change, culminating in an introduction to calculus. The module also introduces differentiation, integration, probability, and statistics.
    

    

      This module is suitable for students with no prior background in A-level mathematics or its equivalent.
    

  


  

    
By the end of this module, you should be able to:

    
    
      
  • Solve problems involving elementary functions, including exponentials, logarithms, and powers
    • 
      
  • Confidently plot a variety of functions, and identify roots and turning points
    • 
      
  • Use and convert between physical units, understand orders of magnitude, and express quantities in standard form
    • 
      
  • Sum infinite series, understand the concept of infinity, and take limits rigorously
    • 
      
  • Understand and apply key probability distributions such as the Poisson, binomial, and normal distributions
    • 
      
  • Solve problems involving calculus, including differentiation and integration
    • 
      
  • Understand and apply first- and second-order differential equations in scientific contexts
    •
